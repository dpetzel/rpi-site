# About

This site is a reference for Raspberry Pi board-level repair. The goal is to collect and organize hardware repair data that is currently scattered across forums, videos, and personal notes into a single searchable resource.

I recently become interested in micro-soldering/electronics repair. I'm a hands-on learning and was trying to decide how I could get some hands on experience. I came up with trying to source broken Pis that I could learn on. A) They are relatively cheap (at least olders models are) and B) No need to waste time with cases and having to take a million screws out of a laptop. I could acquire boards and get right to learning.

After I got my first couple of boards I found there was very little information online that wasn't "they are cheap, just buy a new one". I started collecting my own notes and measurements which quickly become a scattered collection of markdown images and hacked up board images. I opted to try and get a little more organized and hence came up with the idea of this site. 

## Target Audience
This site is intended for folks who like to tinker and want to try and fix their Raspberry PI, even when it doesn't make sense economically.
